**The problem.** General chatbots are great at giving answers — which is exactly what undermines learning. TutorLens is an LLM tutor designed around *productive struggle*: it asks, hints, and explains rather than solving.

**My role.** Led the design and built the prototype (prompting strategy, guardrails, and the classroom-facing UI), then ran the evaluation.

**Outcome.** In a pilot with **120 undergraduates**, students using TutorLens scored **18% higher** on a delayed conceptual post-test than those using a standard answer-giving assistant — with no drop in completion.
